Layout Settings

These settings adjust the way flows are layed out on the map.


Stiffness of Longest Flow: Controls the stiffness of relatively long flows. Lower settings allow longer flows to bend more.

Stiffness of Zero-Length Flow: Controls the stiffness of relatively short flows. Lower settings allow short flows to bend more.

Weight Exponent: Controls the amount of influence distant features have on each other. The higher the setting, the closer together features must be to influence each other.

Repulsion of Start and End Nodes: Controls the strength with which start and end nodes repel flow lines. The higher the setting, the stronger the repelling force.

Move Flows That Cross Nodes: Clicking this button can improve the map layout by identifing flows on the map that touch unrelated nodes and move them off the nodes.

Anti-Torsion Forces: Controls the degree to which flows are permitted to be asymmetrical. The higher the setting, the more semetrical flows will be.

Stiffness Factor for Peripheral Flows: Controls the stiffness of flows towards the outside of the layout. Lower settings allow peripheral flows to bend more.

Canvas Size: Adjusts the size of the canvas. Flows cannot lie outside the canvas border. Click the eye icon to see the canvas.

Flow Rangebox Size: Controls the maximum bend of flows. Click the eye icon to see the rangebox of selected flows. Uncheck the checkbox to remove rangebox restrictions. Note: Removing rangebox restrictions often results in poor flow layouts.
